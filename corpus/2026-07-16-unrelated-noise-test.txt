TITLE: Best Practices for Sourdough Starter Maintenance in Home Kitchens
SOURCE: https://example.com/baking/sourdough-starter-guide
---
Maintaining a healthy sourdough starter requires consistent feeding, proper temperature control, and attention to the culture's activity level. Most home bakers feed their starter once every 24 hours at room temperature, using equal parts flour and water by weight. A well-maintained starter should double in size within 4 to 6 hours of feeding and develop a pleasant, slightly tangy aroma.

Common issues include a starter that separates with a layer of dark liquid on top, known as "hooch," which indicates the culture is hungry and needs feeding sooner. A starter that fails to rise may be too cold, too old, or contaminated with unwanted bacteria.

For bakers who travel frequently, storing the starter in the refrigerator and feeding it weekly is a practical alternative to daily maintenance. When ready to bake again, the starter should be brought back to room temperature and fed twice over 24 hours before use to restore full leavening activity.

Using unbleached, unbromated flour and filtered or dechlorinated water tends to produce the most consistent and vigorous starters, since chlorine and certain flour treatments can inhibit the wild yeast and lactic acid bacteria responsible for fermentation.
